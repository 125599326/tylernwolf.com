1.1 Good Design

The form of the wallet forces the user to reduce what they carry. Its small size makes it easy to carry. Not only does this make for a nice, compact wallet, but it makes you think about what you really need to carry with you on a daily basis. I've minimized the contents to the bare essentials because of my wallet's small size.

1.2 Find the Gap

  • *Spray bottle squeeze handles hurt hands after a while
  • Cutting boards let chopped vegetables fall off the edge
  • Swipe cards don't always swipe on the first try. The swipe slot requires a certain amount of clearance
  • Change is a burden in one's pocket and usually ends up in a jar, unspent
  • Opening a door while carrying something large is nearly impossible
  • Running out of toilet paper is a tragedy. Timing is everything
  • TV remotes are very easy to lose, but very difficult to locate
  • Touch screen keyboards are not an optimal solution. They lead to misspellings. Instead of focusing on autocorrect technology, there must be a better way
  • Sunglass cases are often much larger than they need to be and sometimes cannot be used with different pairs of sunglasses
  • Dusting one's living space is not an enjoyable experience
